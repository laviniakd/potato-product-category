Product Category Annotation Task

You will be shown 25 full-page magazine advertisements from 20th-century American magazines (Time, Esquire, Life, Forbes, Variety). Each ad might be one page or take up a two-page spread. For each ad, select the single best-fitting product category from the list provided.

Category Taxonomy

We use a taxonomy based on the IAB Ad Product Taxonomy 2.0. The 38 product categories cover the full range of advertised products and services; two additional options (⚑ Don't know and ⚑ Not a cohesive ad) are available when needed. If you are unsure which top-level category a specific product belongs to, open the taxonomy link and use your browser's search (Ctrl/Cmd+F) to look up the product name. For example, searching watch reveals that watches fall under Clothing and Accessories:

Guidelines

  • Choose the most specific category that fits the primary product being advertised.
  • Base your judgment on the ad's primary offer, not secondary imagery or context.
  • If an ad promotes a brand that spans multiple categories (e.g. a department store), choose Retail.
  • Tobacco products (cigarettes, cigars, pipe tobacco) → Tobacco, not Consumer Packaged Goods.
  • Alcohol (beer, wine, spirits) → Alcohol, not Food and Beverage Services.
  • Cars, trucks, motorcycles → Vehicles.
  • If you cannot identify a product category from the image, select ⚑ Don't know and optionally explain in the comment box.
  • If the page does not appear to be a coherent single advertisement (e.g. table of contents, editorial page, detection error), select ⚑ Not a cohesive ad.
After these instructions you will see two practice questions with known correct answers. You must answer both correctly to proceed to the main task.
